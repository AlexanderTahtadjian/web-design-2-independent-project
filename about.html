Creating "Elementals"

Artist

Alexander Tahtadjian was born on April 15 1999, the youngest of three children. After graduating from the Graphic Design program at Algonquin College, he continues to further his craft for illustration, graphic design and story telling; while still being taugh by the best teachers and mentors any adolescent could ask for. He is the first youngest winner of the Canada Youth Literary Award given to the most promising new authors. His passions include: drawing, building with LEGO, creating videos, cooking, telling stories, sports and video games. But most of all, he enjoys being with his family and friends as well as helping out others. This is his first big project to ever be created. His first published work was a french short story; the second was a picture book for younger children, and the third was a short comic. His dream is to draw characters for video game, TV, cartoons, animeted shows and movies, while at the same time giving them great backstories and personalities which the audience can relate to. Elementals was his firs big success in bringing this series to life, with a second and third book on the way. With amazing themes and glorious details which appeal to readers of all ages; Elementals has been nominated for several literary prizes and graphic novel rewards, has been translated into 1o different languages and has sold over 4 million copies worldwide.

About The World

The world of "Elementals" is not just your typical standard run of the mill fantasy adventure world. It's a fantasy worl mixed with a few hints of sci-fi thrown in for a more interesting appearence and storytelling experience. Whit inspiration from other great fantasy and sci-fi stories like Lord Of The Rings, Harry Potter, The Chronicles Of Narnia, Star Wars just to name a few. Its also inspired from real historicle events like The 47 Ronin, The tales of Greek Mythology just to name a few; "Elementals" is a world where youn wont only see aspects form both the real and fictional worlds, but also learn that these to realities are more similar then we think.
